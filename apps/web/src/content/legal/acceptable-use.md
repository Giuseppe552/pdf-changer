# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective:** 2026-04-15

This policy defines what you may and may not do with PDF Changer. It is part of and incorporated into the [terms of service](/terms). Violating this policy may result in account suspension, termination, or referral to law enforcement.

## Permitted use

You may use the service for any lawful purpose, including personal, commercial, journalistic, academic, and security-research use. The tools are designed to run locally in your browser — your files stay on your device.

## Prohibited use

You may not use the service to:

### Fraud, forgery, and deception
- Create, alter, or process forged identity documents, certificates, diplomas, or credentials
- Modify documents to misrepresent their origin, authorship, content, or date
- Remove or alter notarial seals, official stamps, or certification marks for deceptive purposes
- Prepare documents intended to deceive any person, organisation, court, or government body

### Copyright
- Remove watermarks, DRM information, or copyright notices from material you do not own or have licence to modify
- Strip metadata from copyrighted works to conceal authorship or ownership
- Reproduce, distribute, or create derivative works from copyrighted material in violation of applicable law

### Tampering with evidence
- Destroy, conceal, alter, or falsify documents relevant to any legal, regulatory, or administrative proceeding
- Process documents with the intent to obstruct justice or impede an investigation

### Illegal content and abuse
- Process or distribute material depicting child sexual abuse or exploitation (CSAM)
- Process content that constitutes a criminal offence under the laws of England and Wales or your own jurisdiction
- Create or process documents used to threaten, harass, stalk, defame, or intimidate anyone
- Impersonate any person or entity

### Attacking the service
- Interfere with, disrupt, or degrade the service or its infrastructure
- Probe or scan for vulnerabilities without written authorisation (responsible disclosure is welcome — see below)
- Use automated tools to access the service beyond reasonable human usage

## Responsible security disclosure

If you discover a security issue in the service, we welcome responsible reports. Contact security@pdfchanger.org with details. Do not publicly disclose the issue before we have had reasonable time to investigate and address it.

## Reporting violations

If you believe someone is using the service in violation of this policy, email abuse@pdfchanger.org. Include as much detail as possible. We review every report.

## Enforcement

We may respond to violations by:

1. Issuing a written warning
2. Temporarily suspending account access
3. Permanently terminating the account without refund
4. Reporting the activity to relevant law enforcement or regulatory authorities
5. Cooperating with valid legal process

The specific response depends on the severity and nature of the violation. We are not required to follow the sequence above — serious violations may result in immediate termination and referral without prior warning.

## Appeals

If you believe your account was suspended or terminated in error, email appeals@pdfchanger.org with your account details and an explanation. We will review the appeal within 14 days. Our decision on appeal is final.

## Changes

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected in the effective date and, where practical, communicated via the site.
